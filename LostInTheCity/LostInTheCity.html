Instructions

Welcome to Katie's "Lost in the City," a fun digital escape adventure for intermediate ESL students!

  1. The Team: Your group will play together as one team, but everyone will need a partner (or group of 3) for some sub-challenges.
  2. Read the Narrative: Students will take turns reading the story of the friends' vacation in New York.
  3. Participate in Challenges: As you read, you'll encounter various challenges. Work collaboratively to solve them and advance through the adventure.
  4. Improve Your English: Engage in listening and speaking activities that enhance your pronunciation, descriptive skills, vocabulary, and more. Communication is key to finding your way back to the hotel.

Remember: This escape adventure is all about having fun while enhancing your English language skills. Work together, communicate effectively, and enjoy the journey through the vibrant streets of New York City!

Good luck, and let the adventure begin!

Are you ready to start?

Our adventure begins...

You and your friends have traveled to New York for a fun vacation, and after a long morning of sightseeing, you realize that you're lost!

You need to find your way to your hotel. Use your English skills to get to your hotel.

First things first; you decide to ask someone for help.

Read the dialogue, focusing on pronunciation:

(Choose two participants. One will be "You" and the other the "NYC Local")

You: Excuse me, do you know where the Crown Plaza Hotel is?

NYC Local: The Crown Plaza? Yes, I do. It's not far from here.

You: Oh great! Could you give me directions please? My phone battery has died and I don't have a map.

NYC Local: Sure thing. So, you're going to turn right at this intersection and walk straight for about three blocks until you reach the roundabout.

You: Roundabout?

NYC Local: Yeah, there's a big circular area with several exits. You need to take the second. There's a signpost that says 'Crown Plaza' next to it.

You: Take the second exit... Got it.

NYC Local: Then, just keep walking along that street for another block or so, and you'll see the hotel on your right-hand side. It's a tall building with lots of windows.

You: Okay, I think I have it. Thank you very much!

NYC Local: No problem. Enjoy your stay in New York!

Dialogue Follow-up Questions (Comprehension)

1. What does the NYC Local mean when they say, "sure thing"?

2. What is a "roundabout"?

3. What is another way to say, "keep walking along"?

Please answer all questions before continuing.

Drawing Challenge

Can you draw a map of the directions the helpful NYC local gave you?

  1. Start
  2. Turn right at "this" intersection
  3. Walk straight for about three blocks
  4. At the roundabout, take the second exit (There's a signpost that says 'Crown Plaza')
  5. Keep walking along that street for another block
  6. The hotel will be on the right-hand side.

Activity: Work with your team to draw this map on paper or a whiteboard. Make sure to include all the landmarks and directions mentioned!

Street Names Challenge

You show your map to the NYC local, and they confirm that everything is correct.

To make sure you don't take a wrong turn, the NYC local gives you some street names that you can add to your map to make it more accurate.

Listen carefully and choose the correct spelling of the street names that you are told:

1. Turn right at this intersection of ______ and ________.

2. Walk straight for about three blocks until you reach the roundabout. You will pass ______, ________, & ________ street.

3. You need to take the second exit of the roundabout, onto __________.

4. Then just keep walking along that street for another block or so, and you'll see the hotel on your right-hand side. It's on ____________.

Please answer all questions before continuing.

Good! Your map is complete.

You now have all the street names added to your map. Time to find your hotel!

Finding the Hotel

You finally find the street your hotel should be on, but you can't find the hotel!

Your friend will ask you some questions about the hotel so that you can all find it faster.

Use your "memory" of the photos of the hotel to help you answer her questions.

Ah! There's the hotel! What a relief.

But, before you go up to your room, you decide to grab a bite to eat first.

Eating food from a street vendor cart is a "must" when you visit New York! So, you follow your noses to a cart that smells delicious.

First, you make sure you know what everything is on the MENU

Now that you're familiar with what is offered, it's time to order.

Activity: Everyone will order something to eat and something to drink from the vendor.

Important: You must also ask at least one question about the menu, for example if you can add or remove an ingredient, or what sauces are offered.

Take turns role-playing as the customer and vendor!
